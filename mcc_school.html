Our Institution

A Legacy of
Excellence in Education

MCC Higher Secondary School is one of Chennai's most storied institutions, founded in 1835 by Rev. George James Laurie and Rev. Matthew Bowie as St. Andrew's School in Egmore. What began as a modest school for boys has grown into a premier educational landmark.

"Conveying as great an amount of truth as possible through the channel of a good education."

Located at its present campus in Chetpet — formerly known as Napier Garden — since 1950, the school blends a rich colonial heritage with modern academic rigour. It is affiliated with the Tamil Nadu State Board and conducts classes from 8 AM to 4 PM daily.

MCC is part of the United States Department of State exchange programme and participates in AFS student exchanges to the United States and Japan, giving students a truly global outlook.

Our Journey

191 Years of History

✦ ✦ ✦
1835

The Founding

Rev. James Laurie and Rev. Matthew Bowie establish St. Andrew's School in Egmore. Rev. John Anderson renames it the General Assembly School, welcoming 59 boys.

1862

Rev. Dr. William Miller

Miller takes the helm, founding Madras Christian College on a 375-acre wooded campus and transforming the institution into a premier South Asian educational centre.

1877

A New Name

The school receives its enduring name: Madras Christian College. Curriculum is broadened and reputation grows across the region.

1950

The Chetpet Campus

Under Mr. Kuruvilla Jacob, the school moves to its current campus at Napier Garden, Chetpet — a landmark moment in its institutional history.

1973–1992

Dr. Clement Felix Era

The swimming pool, auditorium, hostels, the Britannia Amritraj Tennis Academy, and the MRF Pace Foundation are established. Higher Secondary course is introduced.

2006–Present

Modern Era

Dr. G.J. Manohar introduces Smart Board Classes, nursery and primary sections, student exchange programmes, and career guidance — steering MCC into the 21st century.
